# E-commerce Marketing & Sales Analytics Case Study

## Project Overview

This project analyzes an **e-commerce dataset** to understand customer behavior, marketing effectiveness, and sales performance.
The goal is to extract **business insights from transactional data** and recommend strategies to improve **customer acquisition, retention, revenue generation, and marketing ROI**.

The analysis was conducted using **Python and data analysis libraries** to perform exploratory data analysis, customer lifecycle analysis, and marketing performance evaluation.

---

# Dataset Description

The project uses multiple datasets related to e-commerce operations:

1. **Online_sales.csv**
   Contains transaction-level sales data including products purchased, price, quantity, and coupon usage.

2. **CustomersData.xlsx**
   Customer demographic information including gender, location, and tenure.

3. **Marketing_spend.csv**
   Daily marketing expenditure across online and offline channels.

4. **Discount_Coupon.csv**
   Coupon campaigns and discount percentages applied to different product categories.

5. **Tax_amount.xlsx**
   Tax rates applied to various product categories.

# Key Analysis Performed

## 1 Customer Acquisition Analysis

* Identified the **first purchase date for each customer**.
* Analyzed customer acquisition trends across months.
* Found that **January had the highest acquisition**, while **November had the lowest**.

### Business Insight

Customer acquisition shows strong **seasonal patterns**, indicating marketing campaigns and customer purchase behavior vary throughout the year.

---

## 2 Customer Retention Analysis

Retention was calculated using month-to-month repeat purchases.

### Key Finding

* **July showed the strongest retention**
* **January had the lowest retention**

### Business Insight

Retention varies significantly depending on **post-purchase engagement and promotional strategies**.

---

## 3 Revenue Analysis

Revenue was calculated using:

Revenue = Quantity × Average Price

The project compares revenue generated by:

* **New customers**
* **Existing customers**

### Insight

Existing customers provide **stable recurring revenue**, while new customers drive growth spikes.

---

## 4 Coupon & Discount Analysis

The project evaluates how coupon usage impacts revenue.

### Key Findings

* Coupon usage significantly increases overall sales.
* Moderate discounts (around **10%**) drive strong purchase behavior without heavily impacting margins.

---

## 5 Product Performance Analysis

Top-performing products were identified using:

* Total quantity sold
* Total revenue generated

### Insight

A small number of products contribute disproportionately to revenue, suggesting opportunities for:

* product bundling
* targeted promotions
* inventory prioritization

---

## 6 Marketing ROI Analysis

Marketing efficiency was evaluated using:

ROI = (Revenue − Marketing Spend) / Marketing Spend × 100

### Key Insight

Some months generate **significantly higher marketing ROI**, indicating that better campaign timing and targeting can improve profitability.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

* Increase marketing spend during **high-ROI months**
* Focus retention strategies during **low retention periods**
* Use **moderate discount strategies** instead of deep discounts
* Promote **high-performing products** in campaigns
* Implement **category-specific promotions** to maximize revenue
